# Day 58 of 100 — Customer Interviews (Batch 2 of 3)

## 1. Customer Interview Responses — Batch 2 (Next 5)

### Script (same as Day 57)

1. *"Tell me about your property and the land you manage."*
2. *"Have you had issues with trespassing or unauthorized crossing in the past year?"*
3. *"Walk me through the last time it happened. What did you do?"*
4. *"What have you tried to prevent or detect this?"*
5. *"What was wrong with each thing you tried?"*
6. *"If you could wave a magic wand, what would the perfect solution look like?"*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A sensor that texts me when someone is on my land. It doesn't need to take video. It doesn't need to be 4K. It just needs to tell me, right now, that someone is here, so I can call the sheriff while they're still on my property."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"How fast can you get me one? I want it. $120? I'll pay that today. $29 a month? That's less than I spend on coffee. Tell me where to sign up."*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Yes — immediate. Gave credit card info for hardware deposit.

**Notable quote:**
> *"If this works, I'll buy 5. One for each section of the ranch."*

### Interview 8: Sgt. J. W. — Texas Department of Public Safety, Border Security Unit

**Age:** 48 | **Role:** Sergeant, special operations | **New contact** (LinkedIn outreach)

**Past behavior:**
> *"I've been doing border security for 12 years. I've seen every technology come and go. The problem is always the same: you need to know where to put your resources, and you don't know until after something happens."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"We have cameras on towers at major crossings. They cost $50,000 each. We can't put them everywhere. The gaps are where the traffic goes. We need cheap, disposable sensors that tell us where to look."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"We tested seismic sensors, acoustic sensors, thermal drones. They all work, but they all cost too much to deploy at scale. We need something we can put 100 of."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A sensor that costs under $200, lasts 6 months, and tells us if someone walked through a specific area. We don't need video. We need presence detection."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"The analog trigger is interesting. Most sensors fail because they can't filter false positives in the field. If yours actually works with <5 µA idle, that's a game-changer for us. But I need to see test data first."*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Conditional — interested but needs performance data. Mentioned NDAs and security clearance requirements.

**Notable quote:**
> *"Our budget per sensor is $500 max. If you can deliver at $120, I can deploy 100 of them in a single corridor."*

## 2. Key Takeaways

1. **Two pricing tiers emerged from the data:** A $29/mo standard tier and a $15/mo basic tier (lower priority alerting, fewer features) for price-sensitive individuals.
2. **Theft tolerance is a feature.** Commissioner Greg and Sergeant J.W. both said they need a sensor cheap enough that theft is not a disaster. At $120 BOM, we have that.
3. **County budget pathway exists.** Greg P. can approve a pilot without board vote if it is under $5,000.
4. **Implicit demand for larger deployments.** Frank (5 units), Sarah (2 units), Greg (20–50 units) — the unit economics improve at scale.
