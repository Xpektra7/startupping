# Day 52 of 100 — Pricing & Willingness to Pay

## 1. Willingness-to-Pay Research

### Method

Three personas were asked: *"How much would you pay per month for a device that texts you the moment a human crosses your property at night, with no false alerts from animals?"*

Responses were collected after demonstrating the MVP concept and explaining the analog trigger (no false wakes, 4–6 month battery life).

### Results by Persona

#### Tom — The Burned Rancher (NPS: Promoter)

**Stated WTP:** $50/mo after proof. $0/mo before proof.
**Context:** Spent $4,700 on fence repairs and livestock loss in 2024.

> *"If you show me it works — if I get a text and I go out there and catch someone — $50 a month is nothing. That's one less fence repair per year."*

**Maximum acceptable:** $60/mo (if it includes cellular data)
**Minimum threshold for value:** He would stop paying if he went 3 months without a verified alert.

#### Sheriff D. — County Law Enforcement (NPS: Passive-Promoter)

**Stated WTP:** $499/mo for unlimited agency tier. Up to $2,000 for a pilot hardware purchase.

> *"If I can show the board that this led to an arrest, they'll fund it. But I need a pilot first. I can move $2,000 from training."*

**Per-unit equivalent:** $30–$50/mo per sensor at agency scale (20+ units).
**Budget constraint:** Cannot sign a PO over $2,000 without board approval. Hardware must be under $200/unit for direct purchase.

#### Dr. Elena — Conservation Biologist (NPS: Detractor on price)

**Stated WTP:** $10–$15/mo per camera (nonprofit rate).

> *"At $29/mo for 30 cameras, that's more than our entire equipment budget. We need a grant tier."*

**Reality check:** Conservation budgets are 1/3 of commercial. A separate tier or annual pre-pay discount is required to access this segment.

### Comparison with Competitors

| Product | Hardware cost | Monthly cost | Our target price delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reolink Go Ranger PT | $280 | $10–$20 (data only) | Our SaaS is higher but includes AI, alerting, and health telemetry |
| GardePro X66 Pro Max | $200 | $0 (AI free) | Our $29/mo must justify value through reliability and zero false alarms |
| Reconeyez (military) | $2k–$5k | $500–$5k (service) | Our $499/mo agency tier is 10x cheaper |
| Yarn Mesh AI TrailCam | ~$300 | $15/mo (NZD) | Comparable pricing; our analog advantage is the differentiator |
| Campark TC35 | $200 | $10–$20 (data) | Hardware cheaper; but false alarms drain battery and trust |

### Pricing Decision

| Tier | Price | Target | Positioning vs. competition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Single** | $29/mo | Rancher (Tom) | 30-day free trial. Price set below the $50/mo ceiling to reduce friction. |
| **Ranch (5 units)** | $99/mo | Multi-ranch / large landowner | Volume discount; encourages deployment across full property. |
| **Agency (unlimited)** | $499/mo | Sheriff departments | Flat rate for unlimited sensors. Priced at the $2k pilot budget covers 4 months. |
| **Nonprofit** | $12/mo | Conservation reserves | Grant-friendly rate. Requires annual commitment. |

**Hardware:** $120 at-cost (no margin) — this is a deliberate choice to align with the customer's distrust of gadget pricing. Revenue comes from SaaS.

### Key Pricing Insight

The rancher segment will not pay upfront. They have been burned by "too good to be true" gadgets that died in a week. The 30-day free trial is not a marketing tactic — it is a trust requirement.

The sheriff segment does not care about the hardware cost. They care about the total cost of deployment and whether it leads to arrests. Flat-rate agency pricing simplifies their budget approval.

The conservation segment needs grant-friendly pricing. A separate nonprofit tier with annual commitment unlocks a mission-aligned revenue stream that also provides field data for product improvement.

## 2. Prospecting messages
Price-focused pitch to Tom:
`"Your fence repairs cost you $4,700 last year. Our sensor is $29/mo — $348/year. If it catches one event, it pays for itself 10x. Try it free for 30 days."`

## 3. Analog connection
The analog front-end enables the pricing model. Without the <5 µA idle current, the product would require weekly battery swaps and generate enough false alerts to erode user trust within the first month. The low idle current is what makes the $29/mo SaaS model viable — because the hardware actually lasts long enough to justify the recurring fee.
