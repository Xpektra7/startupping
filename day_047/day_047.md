## 3. Analog IC datasheet analysis
Suggested IC: `LM358` (core component of the analog wake front-end)

The LM358 is the heart of the analog trigger. Its key parameters for this application:
- Quiescent current: 350 µA per channel (we use one channel for amplification, one for comparison)
- Supply voltage range: 3–32 V (compatible with 3.3V rail after biasing)
- Input common-mode range: includes ground (critical for single-supply PIR interface)
- Output swing: within 1.5V of rail (drives MOSFET gate directly)
- Slew rate: 0.3 V/µs (fast enough for PIR signal bandwidth of ~10 Hz)

In the BorderGuard AI trigger, the LM358 runs continuously at 350 µA — the dominant component of the <5 µA budget (with the rest going to the PIR element and bias network). The total analog front-end consumes less power than the self-discharge rate of the battery.
