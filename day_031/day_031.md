## 3. Market the hardware product
Product: `EV Battery Management System (EV BMS)`

Post idea:
The BMS calculates state of charge (SOC) using coulomb counting and voltage-based correction.

Coulomb counting integrates current over time, while voltage correction compensates for drift and reset errors.

The combination gives a reliable estimate of remaining energy in the pack.

## 4. Circuit scenario + SPICE
Scenario:
An active cell balancing circuit using a switched capacitor.

Post idea:
Active balancing transfers energy between cells instead of wasting it as heat. A switched-capacitor balancer uses capacitors and MOSFET switches to shuttle charge between adjacent cells.

SPICE can be used to study charge transfer efficiency, balancing current, switching losses, and settling time. This approach is more efficient than passive balancing but requires more complex control logic.

## 6. Analog IC datasheet analysis
Suggested IC: `LTC3300-1`

Technical idea:
The `LTC3300-1` is a fault-protected bidirectional active cell balancer IC designed for high-voltage battery stacks.

Key datasheet parameters to analyze:
- Maximum balancing current per cell
- Bidirectional energy transfer topology
- Fault protection features (overcurrent, overtemperature)
- Communication interface (SPI daisy chain)
- Operating voltage range per cell
- Efficiency across cell voltage mismatch

In an EV BMS, the LTC3300-1 enables high-efficiency active balancing that reduces thermal load and improves pack usable capacity compared to passive resistor-based balancing.
