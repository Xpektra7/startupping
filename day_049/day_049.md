# Day 49 of 100 — Customer Interviews

## 1. Customer Interview Responses

### Interview 1: Tom H. — Rancher, Cochise County, AZ (3,200 acres, cattle)

**Age:** 58
**Land:** Family ranch since 1972
**Pain level:** High (cut fence 4 times in 2024, lost 2 calves)

> *"I've tried everything. Solar lights, game cameras that send pictures — they die in a week. The batteries can't keep up. I'm not interested in another gadget unless it actually lasts."*

**Reaction to MVP concept:**
Tom lit up when I explained the analog trigger. He immediately understood why it would save battery.

> *"Wait — so it doesn't record the deer? It just watches for people? That's what I need. I don't care about the deer. I care about the guys cutting my fence at 2 AM."*

**Objections:**
- **Cellular coverage:** "I barely get one bar at the house. Will it work down in the wash?"
- **Theft:** "How do I know somebody won't just steal the camera?"

**Price sensitivity:**
> *"If it works — if I get a text and I go out there and catch someone — I'd pay $50 a month. But I need to see it work first."*

**Verdict:** Strong yes, contingent on proof. Cellular coverage is a real concern.

---

### Interview 2: Deputy R. M. — Santa Cruz County Sheriff's Office (17 years)

**Role:** Patrol deputy, frequently assigned to southern corridor
**Pain level:** Moderate-High (frustrated by inability to respond in time)

> *"We get calls from ranchers saying 'my fence is cut again.' By the time I get there — 45 minutes minimum — there's nobody. I write a report and leave. It feels useless."*

**Reaction to MVP concept:**
Deputy M. was immediately interested but asked the hard questions.

> *"So it texts the rancher. What happens then? The rancher calls us. Now we have a first-hand witness and a location. That's better than anything we have now."*

**Objections:**
- **Integration:** "Can we get the alert directly? If we have to rely on the rancher to call us, that's a delay."
- **False alerts:** "If it goes off every night for a cow, my dispatcher will hate me."

**Price sensitivity:**
> *"For a pilot, we might have grant money. But it has to be under $2,000 for me to write a PO without supervisor sign-off. If it's $120 like you say, we can do that."*

**Verdict:** Enthusiastic about the concept, needs agency-level alert integration for full value.

---

### Interview 3: Dr. Elena V. — Conservation Biologist, Organ Pipe Cactus National Monument

**Education:** PhD Wildlife Ecology
**Pain level:** Moderate (frustrated by passive tools, but not her budget)

> *"We have 30 trail cameras out there. In theory, someone checks them every two weeks. In practice, it's more like a month. We found a poached desert bighorn last year — the carcass was completely decomposed."*

**Reaction to MVP concept:**
Dr. V. was intrigued but cautious.

> *"The real-time alert is valuable, but our biggest problem is coverage. We need 50 cameras to cover the monument. Can you do $50 per camera per year? That's what we pay for data plans now."*

**Objections:**
- **Budget:** "We're grant-funded. Our equipment budget is fixed for the fiscal year."
- **False positives:** "If it alerts for hikers who are legally on the trail, we can't use it."
- **OTA updates:** "We can't physically access cameras in the backcountry for firmware updates."

**Price sensitivity:**
> *"At $29/mo per camera, that's $348/year. We have 30 cameras. That's $10,440 — our entire equipment budget. You'd need to be closer to $10/mo for us to scale."*

**Verdict:** Mission-fit but priced above conservation budgets. Suggests a different tier or grant-funded pricing model.

## 2. Key Takeaways

| Theme | Action |
|---|---|
| Cellular coverage is a top concern | Add external antenna option + LoRa fallback |
| Unit theft is a blocker | Add tamper alarm + geofence alert if unit moves |
| Ranchers need proof before paying | MVP must be free 30-day trial with no commitment |
| Sheriffs want direct alerts | Add agency-level notification forwarding to the roadmap |
| Conservation budgets are tight | Explore lower-tier pricing ($10–$15/mo) for NGOs |
