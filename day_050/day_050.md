# Day 50 of 100 — Ideal Customer Profile

## 1. Ideal Early Adopter Profile

### Primary: The Land-Limited Rancher

| Attribute | Detail | Source / Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Age** | 45–65 | Owns land free and clear. Has 15–25 years of operating experience. Skeptical of tech but practical. |
| **Location** | U.S. border counties: Cochise (AZ), Santa Cruz (AZ), Hudspeth (TX), Brewster (TX), Hidalgo (NM). Within 20 miles of the Mexico border. | These counties account for the highest density of border-crossing damage incidents on private land. |
| **Income** | $60k–$120k/yr household | Land-rich but cash-tight. The $500–$5k fence repair hits the operating budget hard. |
| **Profession** | Multi-generation cattle rancher or large-parcel farmer | Not a hobby farmer. Their livelihood depends on the land and they cannot relocate. |
| **Land size** | 500–5,000 acres | Too large to patrol on foot, too small to justify a security contractor. |
| **Pain tenure** | 5–15 years of recurring trespass damage | This is not a new problem. They have tried multiple solutions and none have worked. |
| **Smartphone** | Android, mid-range (Moto G, Samsung A series), 3–4 years old | Uses it for calls, texts, weather, cattle auction prices. No smart home, no automation. |
| **Digital habits** | Checks phone 3–5x/day. Does not use apps beyond texting, weather, banking. | Will not download a dedicated dashboard app. Must work via SMS. |
| **Social proof** | Knows 5–15 other ranchers in the same county. They talk at feed stores, coffee shops, county extension meetings. | One verified detection spreads by word of mouth faster than any ad campaign. |
| **Buying trigger** | A specific costly event: cut fence, stolen water pump, livestock loss | The week after a $1,500 repair bill is the only time they will open their wallet for a new solution. |
| **Willingness to pay** | $30–$50/mo, after seeing proof | Will not pay upfront for an unproven product. 30-day free trial is essential. |
| **Willingness to install** | Moderate — will mount a unit on a T-post if instructions are simple | Not technical, but mechanically competent (they fix fences, drive tractors, weld). |
| **What they will NOT do** | Will not: configure WiFi, update firmware, pair Bluetooth, download a dashboard, set up geofences | The product must work with zero configuration beyond mounting and turning on. |

---

### Why This Profile Specifically

The multi-generation rancher is the ideal early adopter because:

1. **They have the most urgent pain.** A cut fence costs real money — $500–$5k per incident — and the emotional frustration is immense. They feel abandoned by the system.

2. **They have no alternatives that work.** Military systems are too expensive. Consumer cameras die in days. Calling the sheriff does nothing.

3. **They are geographically concentrated.** Border counties in AZ, NM, and TX have the same problem, the same demographic, and the same communication channels. A single county pilot reaches the entire segment.

4. **They talk to each other.** Ranchers share information constantly. One unit that catches a real event becomes the topic of conversation at the feed store and sells the next three.

5. **They are practical decision-makers.** No procurement committee. No grant application. If they see it work and the price is right, they buy it with a personal credit card.

### Profiles to Ignore (For Now)

| Profile | Reason |
|---|---|
| Homeowners near the border | Lot too small, too dispersed, low individual pain |
| Hunters / recreationists | Want wildlife photos, not security. Different product. |
| Large farms with hired security | Already solved the problem with budget |
| Border Patrol / CBP | Too long a sales cycle, requires government contracting |

## 2. Prospecting messages
Tailored for this profile:

`"I see you had fence damage on [road name] last month. I'm testing a $120 sensor that texts you the moment someone crosses your land at night — with no false alerts from deer or cows. Want to try it for free for 30 days? You keep the unit."`

## 3. Analog connection
The <5 µA analog front-end is what makes the price point and the field life possible. A rancher will not pay $50/mo if he has to climb a ladder to replace batteries every week. The analog trigger is not a feature — it is the enabler of the entire business model.
