# BorderGuard AI

> Low-cost, AI-powered border surveillance built on a **sub-5 µA analog wake front-end**. Standard trail cameras + a discrete BJT/comparator trigger + localized edge AI = real-time intrusion alerts for a fraction of military hardware.

---

## Who Has This Problem Most Urgently

### The Ideal Early Adopter

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 45–65 |
| **Location** | Southern Arizona, New Mexico, West Texas — within 20 miles of the US–Mexico border |
| **Income** | $60k–$120k/year household (asset-rich, cash-tight — owns land, not liquidity) |
| **Profession** | Multi-generation rancher, cattle operator, or large-parcel farmland owner |
| **Land size** | 500–5,000 acres |
| **Problem tenure** | Has been cutting fence-repair costs out of operating budget for 5–15 years |
| **Digital habits** | Owns a smartphone (Android, mid-range). Uses it for calls, texts, weather, cattle prices. Does not use smart home tech, does not own a tablet. Checks phone 3–5x/day. |
| **Willingness to pay** | High — if it works. Skeptical of tech promises. Will pay $30–50/mo after seeing a neighbor's unit catch a real event. Will not pay for a dashboard, analytics, or "insights." Pays for **alerts that prevent damage.** |
| **Trigger buying** | After a specific costly event (cut fence cost $2k in cattle loss; water pump stolen; shed broken into). |

### Why Them Specifically

This profile has the most urgent pain because:

1. **They absorb the damage directly.** Unlike a sheriff (who logs a report and leaves), the rancher pays for every broken fence, every stolen pump, every stressed cow. A single breach costs $500–$5,000.
2. **They have no alternative.** Military/contractor surveillance starts at $15k–$50k upfront. Cellular trail cameras ($200–$400) drain batteries in 3–6 days trying to send video of tumbleweeds. Neither works.
3. **Their land is a predictable corridor.** Trespassers follow the same washes, fence gaps, and cattle paths. A single well-placed unit covers the bottleneck.
4. **They talk to each other.** Ranchers share what works. One verified detection at the feed store or coffee shop sells the next three units.

| **Digital habits** | Uses MDT in squad car. Personal phone for off-duty. Not an early tech adopter. | Uses field tablet/GPS. Familiar with camera traps. Frustrated with offline gear. |
| **Willingness to pay** | Moderate — procurement cycles are 6–12 months and require a purchase order under $5k. Needs to see a pilot first. | Low–moderate — grants available but slow. Needs proof of effectiveness in similar terrain. |

### Who Should Be Ignored (For Now)

- **Homeowners near the border** — too dispersed, low individual land value, small lots
- **Hikers and recreationists** — no recurring revenue model
- **Large military contractors** — not our market; they sell $50k towers
- **"Tech enthusiasts"** — want an API, a mobile app, and integration with Home Assistant. They will cost more in support than they pay.
