## 4. Circuit scenario + SPICE
Scenario:
An undervoltage lockout (UVLO) circuit for BMS power supply.

Post idea:
The BMS electronics must turn off cleanly when the battery voltage drops below a safe threshold. An undervoltage lockout circuit prevents the control electronics from operating in an undefined state.

A practical UVLO uses a comparator with hysteresis, a precision reference, and a resistor divider. SPICE can be used to verify the turn-on and turn-off thresholds, hysteresis band, response time, and supply current.

This circuit prevents logic glitches and protects the battery from deep discharge damage.

## 6. Analog IC datasheet analysis
Suggested IC: `TLV7011`

Technical idea:
The `TLV7011` is a low-voltage comparator suitable for UVLO and threshold detection in BMS power management.

Key datasheet parameters to analyze:
- Supply voltage range and quiescent current
- Input offset voltage and hysteresis accuracy
- Propagation delay and output rise/fall time
- Input common-mode voltage range
- Push-pull or open-drain output options
- Operating temperature range

In a UVLO circuit, the comparator must switch cleanly with minimal chatter, consume very little power, and maintain stable thresholds over temperature.
