# Day 59 of 100 — Customer Interviews (Batch 3 of 3)

## 1. Customer Interview Responses — Batch 3 (Last 5)

### Script

1. *"Tell me about your property and the land you manage."*
2. *"Have you had issues with trespassing or unauthorized crossing in the past year?"*
3. *"Walk me through the last time it happened. What did you do?"*
4. *"What have you tried to prevent or detect this?"*
5. *"What was wrong with each thing you tried?"*
6. *"If you could wave a magic wand, what would the perfect solution look like?"*

### Interview 11: Walt B. — Wildlife Refuge Manager, Buenos Aires NWR, AZ

**Age:** 55 | **Role:** U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service, manages 118,000 acres | **New contact** (cold email via refuge website)

**Past behavior:**
> *"We have 60 trail cameras deployed across the refuge. They are checked on a rotating schedule — some every 2 weeks, some every month. We have documented illegal crossings, drug trafficking, and abandoned vehicles. Most detections are weeks old."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"Budget is frozen. We can't buy new equipment without a specific grant. Our existing cameras are aging. The ones that break don't get replaced. We're losing coverage every year."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"We tested 5 cellular trail cameras in 2022. They were Reolink. They ran through batteries in 3–5 days of normal use. The solar panels helped a little but not enough. We returned them."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A camera that costs under $150, runs for 6 months without maintenance, and alerts us the moment a vehicle or person enters the restricted zone. Integration with our existing mapping system would be ideal."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"The battery life is the key. If you can truly deliver 6 months on solar, I can write that into a grant application. The $29/mo is fine if it includes the data plan. But I need a letter of support for the grant — can you provide that?"*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Grant-dependent. Needs a vendor letter for USFWS grant application.

**Notable quote:**
> *"We're a government agency. We can't buy things that don't exist yet. But if you have a working prototype, we can do a cooperative research agreement."*

### Interview 13: Nora S. — Solar Installation Contractor, Las Cruces, NM

**Age:** 38 | **Services:** Off-grid solar for remote properties | **New contact** (Google search + cold call)

**Past behavior:**
> *"I install solar systems on ranches and off-grid homes. About half my clients ask me about security cameras. They want something that runs on solar and works without WiFi. I don't have a good product to recommend."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"The cellular cameras on the market are junk. They drain batteries. The AI is a gimmick. I've stopped recommending them because I get callbacks complaining. It's bad for my reputation."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"I tried Reolink and Campark. Both failed. The Reolink customer told me it lasted 4 days. The Campark lasted 6. Both said the false alerts were unbearable."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A product that actually works. I don't care about the brand. I care that I can install it and not get a call 2 weeks later saying it died."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"If your battery life claim is real, I will sell this to every single client. I do 50–60 solar installs a year. Almost all of them ask about security. You need a dealer program."*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Yes — wants to be an installer/reseller. Offered to install units on his next 5 solar jobs for free to test.

**Notable quote:**
> *"I make my money on installs. If the hardware is $120 and I can install it for $80 labor, that's a good day. I don't need margin on the subscription — I just need a product that doesn't make me look bad."*

### Interview 14: Hank Y. — Retired Border Patrol Agent, Consultant, Tucson, AZ

**Age:** 67 | **Current role:** Advises tech startups on border security | **New contact** (referred by Sgt. J. W.)

**Past behavior:**
> *"I spent 25 years in the Border Patrol. I've seen every sensor technology fail. The problem is always the same: false positives. You deploy 100 sensors, and the system generates 10,000 alerts a day. Within a week, nobody is looking at the alerts."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"The gap in the market is not better sensors. The gap is sensors that are cheap enough to deploy at scale and smart enough to not generate noise. Every vendor promises low false alarms. None deliver."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"I've evaluated Reconeyez, Defendec, laser tripwires, ground radar, acoustic sensors. They all work in the lab. They all fail in the field. The environment is too harsh."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A sensor that costs under $200, runs for a year, and generates less than 5 false alerts per month. If someone builds that, they win the market."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"The analog approach is interesting. You're solving the false-alert problem at the physical layer instead of with software. That's fundamentally different from what everyone else is doing. I'd like to see your test data. If it works, I'll introduce you to my contacts at CBP."*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Advisory. Offered to review test plan and provide feedback. Can open doors at federal level if results are solid.

**Notable quote:**
> *"Don't sell to CBP yet. You're not ready. Sell to ranchers. Get 100 units in the field. Prove the false-alert rate. Then come back to me and I'll open doors."*

### Interview 15: Candace W. — Property Manager, Large Agribusiness, Yuma County, AZ

**Age:** 43 | **Role:** Security manager for 15,000-acre vegetable operation | **New contact** (cold email)

**Past behavior:**
> *"We have 15,000 acres of produce. Trespassers cut our irrigation lines, steal produce, and sometimes set fires. Last year we lost an estimated $50,000 to theft and damage. We have 3 security guards — they can't cover 15,000 acres."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"We've tried everything. Guard patrols (too expensive, coverage gaps), fence sensors (too many false alarms from wildlife), cameras (need WiFi, no WiFi in the fields). Nothing scales."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"We contracted with a security company that installed 20 cellular cameras. $60,000 upfront. They worked for 2 weeks. Then the batteries died and the false alerts overwhelmed the monitoring center. They pulled the contract."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"I need a sensor that works at this scale. 100 sensors across 15,000 acres. Each sensor costs less than $200. They run on solar. They text my team when someone enters the field. False alerts can't exceed 1 per day per 10 sensors."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"At $120 per unit, I can deploy 100 sensors for $12,000. That's a fraction of what I spent on the failed camera system. The $499/mo agency plan is fine. But I need a 60-day pilot with a service-level agreement."*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Yes — large-scale commercial interest. Needs SLA and volume pricing.

**Notable quote:**
> *"If this works, my budget next year is $200,000 for perimeter security. You could own that entire budget."*
