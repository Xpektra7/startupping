## 3. Market the hardware product
Product: `EV Battery Management System (EV BMS)`

Post idea:
The BMS communicates with external systems through connectors that are exposed to the environment.

These communication lines are vulnerable to electrostatic discharge, surge events, and wiring faults.

Robust interface protection is essential for field reliability.

## 4. Circuit scenario + SPICE
Scenario:
An ESD and surge protection circuit for BMS communication lines.

Post idea:
BMS communication lines (CAN, I2C, SPI, sensor inputs) must survive ESD strikes, surge transients, and short circuits without damaging the controller.

A practical protection circuit uses TVS diodes, series resistors, and common-mode chokes. SPICE can be used to model the clamping voltage, surge current distribution, and residual energy reaching the protected IC under standard ESD and surge waveforms.

The protection design must balance clamping voltage against signal integrity for the communication baud rate.

## 6. Analog IC datasheet analysis
Suggested IC: `TPS2360` / `TVS diode array`

Technical idea:
A TVS diode array such as the `TPS2360` or equivalent can be evaluated for protecting BMS communication interfaces from ESD and surge events.

Key datasheet parameters to analyze:
- Clamping voltage at peak pulse current
- Reverse standoff voltage vs. signal voltage
- Peak pulse power and current rating
- Response time and dynamic resistance
- Capacitance per channel (affects signal integrity)
- Number of protected lines and package size

In a BMS, the protection IC must clamp fast transients without introducing signal degradation that could cause communication errors on the CAN or isoSPI bus.
