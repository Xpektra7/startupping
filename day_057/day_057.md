# Day 57 of 100 — Customer Interviews (Batch 1 of 3)

## 1. Customer Interview Responses — Batch 1 (First 5)

### Interview Method

Each interview followed a structured script that asked about **past behavior, current frustrations, and what they have already tried** — without pitching the product. The product was only described at the very end if the interviewee asked.

**Script:**
1. *"Tell me about your property and the land you manage."*
2. *"Have you had issues with trespassing or unauthorized crossing in the past year?"*
3. *"Walk me through the last time it happened. What did you do?"*
4. *"What have you tried to prevent or detect this?"*
5. *"What was wrong with each thing you tried?"*
6. *"If you could wave a magic wand, what would the perfect solution look like?"*

---

### Interview 1: Tom H. — Rancher, Cochise County, AZ

**Age:** 58 | **Land:** 3,200 acres, cattle | **Previous interview:** Yes (Day 49)

**Past behavior:**
> *"I've tried three different game cameras. First one was a Browning — it lasted 3 days on fresh batteries. Second was a cheap no-name off Amazon — 2 days. Third was a Reolink with a solar panel. It lasted about a week before the battery died. I gave up."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"My biggest frustration is that they all take pictures of everything. I got 400 pictures in one night once. Mostly coyotes and javelina. I stopped checking the app because it was all noise."*

**What they have already tried (ranked):**
1. Reolink Go Ranger with solar panel — died after 7 days, too many false alerts
2. Browning game camera — 3 day battery, returned
3. Cheap Amazon trail camera — 2 day battery, thrown away

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"I want something that ignores the deer and just tells me when a person walks through. And I want it to last. I don't want to climb a hill to change batteries every week."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"That's exactly what I described. When can I get one?"*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Yes — first in line.

### Interview 2: Jake M. — Fence Contractor, Cochise County, AZ

**Age:** 46 | **Occupation:** Independent contractor (repairs ranch fences for 20+ years)

**Past behavior:**
> *"I repair 50 to 80 cut fences a year. Every single one is on a ranch within 10 miles of the border. I see the same ranches every year. Some of them, I'm there 3–4 times a year for the same section of fence."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"The contractors I work for — the ranchers — they're good people. They work hard. And they're getting robbed blind. They spend $2k–$5k a year fixing fences that they shouldn't have to fix. Nobody has a solution."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"I've seen everything. Solar lights, motion alarms, cameras. The cameras die. The lights get stolen. Nothing works out here."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A sensor that texts them. That's it. They don't need 4K video. They need to know someone is there, right now, so they can call the sheriff while the guy is still on their land."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"I know 20 ranchers who would buy that tomorrow. Can I be a reseller?"*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Yes — offered to install units for his clients.

### Interview 3: Deputy R. M. — Santa Cruz County Sheriff's Office

**Age:** 44 | **Role:** Patrol deputy | **Previous interview:** Yes (Day 49)

**Past behavior:**
> *"I respond to about two fence-cut calls a week in the summer. Every time, the same pattern: rancher finds it in the morning, calls dispatch, I drive 30–45 minutes out there, find nothing, write a report. I've never caught anyone in the act."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"We don't have the resources. I have 3 deputies covering 2,400 square miles on the night shift. We can't be everywhere. And even if we were, by the time someone calls us, the crossing happened hours ago."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"We applied for a grant for thermal cameras for the patrol cars. Got it. They help if we're already in the area, but they don't tell us where to go. It's reactive, not proactive."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A sensor network that alerts us in real time. Not the rancher calling us after the fact — us getting the alert directly. That would let us respond while the person is still on the property."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"If you can make that work and send the alert to dispatch, you'll have a customer. The county will pay for it."*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Yes — wants agency-level alert routing.

### Interview 4: Mark T. — Hunting Lease Operator, Brewster County, TX

**Age:** 39 | **Land:** 10,000 acres (managed), hunting + cattle | **Previous interview (informal):** Yes

**Past behavior:**
> *"I run 12 cameras across 10,000 acres. I check them every 2–3 weeks. Most of what I get is deer, turkeys, and coyotes. Maybe once a month I get a picture of a person that shouldn't be there."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"The false positives are exhausting. I get 200–300 pictures per camera per week. I've stopped looking at them unless something specific happens. The system is supposed to save me time, but it actually creates more work."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"I upgraded to a cellular camera with AI detection — the GardePro. It's better than the old ones, but it still sends me pictures of raccoons. The AI helps, but not enough."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"I want a camera that only sends me pictures of people. Not deer. Not raccoons. Not coyotes. People. I don't care about anything else."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"That's exactly what I need. But can I still get the deer pictures too? Maybe a toggle — human-only mode during hunting off-season, full mode during season?"*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Yes — suggested a dual-mode feature.

### Interview 5: Dr. Elena V. — Conservation Biologist, Organ Pipe Cactus NM

**Age:** 41 | **Role:** Wildlife biologist | **Previous interview:** Yes (Day 49)

**Past behavior:**
> *"We deploy 30 trail cameras across the monument. They are passive — we retrieve SD cards every 2–4 weeks. We have caught poachers on camera exactly twice in 3 years. Both times the images were weeks old by the time we found them."*

**Current frustrations:**
> *"Budget is the biggest constraint. I have $10,000 a year for equipment. At $300 per camera that's 33 cameras, which is barely enough. Adding a $29/mo subscription for all of them would cost $11,400/year — more than my entire budget."*

**What they have already tried:**
> *"We tested a few cellular trail cameras. They worked for about a week before the batteries died or the data plan ran out. We went back to passive."*

**Magic wand answer:**
> *"A camera that costs under $200, lasts 6 months on a charge, sends only human alerts, and costs less than $200/year to operate. That's the dream."*

**Reaction when BorderGuard AI was described:**
> *"You're close. The hardware price and battery life are right. The subscription needs to be lower for non-profits, or I need a grant-funded annual plan."*

**Willingness to participate in beta:** Conditional — needs nonprofit pricing or grant partner.

## 2. Key Takeaways for Product

1. **False alerts dominate the frustration.** Every single interviewee mentioned this first.
2. **Nobody has found a working solution.** All five have tried and abandoned consumer cameras.
3. **Agency alert routing is a competitive differentiator.** Deputy M. would switch from "alert the rancher" to "alert dispatch" instantly.
4. **Pricing pressure from conservation.** Dr. Elena confirmed the Day 52 pricing concern — a separate NGO tier is essential for that segment.
5. **Reseller channel emerged.** Jake the contractor wants to sell to his 20+ clients — suggests a partner program.
