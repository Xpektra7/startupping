## 4. Circuit scenario + SPICE
Scenario:
A battery fuel gauge circuit using coulomb counting.

Post idea:
Coulomb counting requires precise current measurement integrated over time. A dedicated fuel gauge IC measures the voltage across a sense resistor and accumulates the charge.

SPICE can be used to model the current sense amplifier, the integration behavior, and the effects of offset error and drift on the accumulated charge estimate over multiple charge-discharge cycles.

The key challenge is that offset errors accumulate, so periodic voltage-based correction is needed to reset the estimate.

## 6. Analog IC datasheet analysis
Suggested IC: `LTC2943`

Technical idea:
The `LTC2943` is a multi-cell battery fuel gauge IC that can be evaluated for coulomb counting and state-of-charge tracking in EV BMS designs.

Key datasheet parameters to analyze:
- Coulomb counter resolution and accumulated charge accuracy
- Sense voltage range and minimum detectable current
- Quiescent current in active and sleep modes
- I2C interface and register map
- Temperature measurement and compensation
- Programmable alert thresholds

In an EV BMS, the LTC2943 provides continuous charge tracking that complements voltage-based SOC estimation, but the designer must manage drift through periodic open-circuit voltage calibration.
